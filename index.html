About Me

I'm Binbin (Emily), an MS Computer Science student at NYU Courant, sitting at the intersection of AI research and production engineering.

My work usually starts with a question I can't let go of. Building a web navigation agent, I hit a ceiling — the agent kept failing not because of bad logic, but because it couldn't anticipate consequences. That led me to training a world model from scratch, synthesizing data, fine-tuning with RL, and eventually building an inference framework that runs 4× faster than tree search. One problem, chased all the way down.

On the applied side, I'm currently building a production risk scoring system for a fintech client — figuring out where AI judgment can replace manual workflows, where it still needs a human in the loop, and how the system can improve itself over time.

Outside of work, I ride bikes and go wherever the road takes me.
